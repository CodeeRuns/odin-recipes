Baked Spaghetti

Description

This is an easy weeknight dish that is quick to throw together and promises to be a hit. Once it has been baked, the spaghetti casserole can be sliced into neat squares, and the layer of ricootta and parmesan filling give it the rich flavours as that of lasagna. You can play around with the recipe for substituting the meat with another, such as Italian sausage or ground turkey, to suit any potential picky eaters.

If you are planning on making this dish in advance, you can assemble the casserole (up until step 5) and then store covered in the refrigerator, until ready to bake. Serve with a side of fresh garden salad to cut through some of the richness of the dish

Ingredients

  • Unsalted butter, for greasing the pan
  • Kosher salt and black pepper
  • 1 pound spaghetti
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 large yellow onion (chopped)
  • 5 cloves garlic (minced)
  • 1 teaspoon dried rosemarry or thyme
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1/4 teaspoon red-pepper flakes, more to taste
  • 1 pound ground beef
  • 1 jar marinara sauce
  • 1/2 cup fresh basil, chopped, plus more for serving
  • 1 cup ricotta cheese
  • 1/4 cup grated parmesan
  • 1 large egg
  • 2 cups grated grated mozzarella

Steps

  1. Heat oven to 350 degrease and grease baking dish with butter. Bring large potof salted water to the boil. Add spaghetti and cook accordingly.
  2. While water comes to boil, heat the olive oil in a large, heavy-bottomed pot over medium heat. Add the onion and cook, stirring occasionally, until tender. Add the garlic, thyme, rosemarry, orgeno, red-pepper, salt, black-pepper, and cook until fragrant
  3. Add the beef and cook, breaking up the meat with a wooden spoon, until browned. Turn off the heat and stir in the marinara sauce and basil. Drain the spaghetti, and add it to the sauce and toss well
  4. In a small bowl, combine the ricotta, 1/2 cup of the parmesan, the egg, and some salt. Mix with a fork until smooth.
  5. Transfer half the pasta and sauce to the prepared baking dish and smooth it into an even layer. Dollop the ricotta mixture onto the pasta in large spoonfuls, then sprinkle 1 cup of the mozzarella on top. Add the rest of the pasta and sauce to the pan, again smoothing it into an even layer.
  6. Cover the pan with aluminium foil and bake for 40 minutes. Sprinkle the remaining 1 cup mozzarella and the remaining 1/4 cup parmesan on top, then bake, uncovered, until the mozeralla has melted.
  7. Cool for 5 minutes, then serve with more chopped basil, a pinch of dried oregano and more red-pepper flakes. Slice the baked spaghetti into large squares and serve hot or warm.
